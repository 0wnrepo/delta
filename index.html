How it works

There are quite a few bitcoin term contract services on the market currently, but they all share the same flaw - most of them rely on a single entity to secure the contracts. While this may be good for gambling and speculation, it won't allow you to build a business that relies on such contracts.

Funds and contracts made within Orisi Delta are stored on a multi signature bitcoin address, handled by a set of trusted entities. Even if we go out of business, the funds will be secure as long as most of the entities mantain their integrity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the e.t.a?

The project is currently under developement with a beta launch planned for the mid October 2014. We expect to gradually increase the security of the system & test it in battle all through the 2015. We're looking for the community feedback, and for people willing to run oracles. Please follow @orisidelta for updates.

What kind of options will be supported?

At launch we plan to support put/call options for USD/BTC, with other sources coming very soon. Please let us know your use case.

Why should I trust you?

Well, you shouldn't. Even our motto says that you shouldn't trust us, and our terms of service explicitly state that you should proceed with an assumption that we're a bunch of incompetent, malicious, idiots (cretins?).

What you should do instead is either verify the system yourself, or rely on experts you know telling you that the system is secure even if we wanted to take the money and run.

What's your security model?

The security model is roughly explained within the video. For more detailed information on Orisi smart contracts, you might want to check out mastering oracles.

But in brief - there are centralised aspects of the system (like this website), but contracts themselves are managed by a set of up to 15 independent arbiters, all running an open source, auditable, code. As long as the arbiters can be trusted to not change the code, and the code can be audited and tested against critical security flaws, everything should be fine.

Did you say 'code not containing critical security flaws'?

Yes, the code is our single point of failure. But - as bitcoin itself shows - with enough eyes looking at the code, ultimately it can grow secure enough to store billions' of dollars of wealth. Our basic code security idea is as follows: - during the initial testing we'll provide a bounty for breaking into the system, - the oracle code will have a publicly accessible source - we hope that community will help out with testing, - just like with Bitcoin - we expect breakins and fund losses happen at the beginning stage of system operation, but with time the system should become more and more robust.

Did you say "trusted entities"?

We plan to have a list of diverse individuals who are more or less trustworthy. In the perfect scenario, we would like some of the arbiters to be people with a high WOT ranking, some trusted by bitcointalk forum, some by /r/bitcoin, some bitcoin company owners, representatives of Bitcoin organizations, and perhaps a few real-world officials. While it would be possible for some of them to begin cheating, for the system to fall apart, over 50% of them would need to cheat simultaneously, without any indication of such action beforehand. We believe it to be highly unlikely, but obviously - the time will tell.
Of course will start with a modest list of oracles - probably containing just the Orisi founders, which is not secure at all. But the list will be expanded with time.
